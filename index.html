Pasta recipe

  1. Boil water in a large pot
  2. To make sure pasta doesnot stick together, use at least 4 quarts of water for every pound of noodles.
  3. Salt the water with at least a tablespoon more is fine
  4. The salty water adds flavor to the pasta
  5. Add pasta
  6. Pour pasta into boiling water. Dont break the pasta it will soften up within 30 seconds and fit into the pot.
  7. Stir the pasta
  8. As the pasta starts to cook, stir it well with the tongs so the noodles dont stick to each other
  9. Test the pasta by tasting it
  10. Follow the cooking time on the package, but always taste pasta before draining to make sure the texture is right. Pasta cooked properly should be al dentea little chewy.
  11. Drain the pasta
  12. Drain cooked pasta well in a colander. If serving hot, add sauce right away

Poha recipe

  1. Wash and soak poha in water for 10 minutes, until they are soft.
  2. Drain and keep aside.
  3. Heat oil in a pan.
  4. When the oil is hot, add mustard seeds.
  5. When the seeds starts splattering, add curry leaves and peanuts.
  6. Fry for 2-3 minutes.
  7. Add onion and fry for a minute.
  8. Add green peas, salt and turmeric powder.
  9. Fry again for a minute.
  10. Add the drained poha and simmer the heat.
  11. Cook for 3-4 minutes.
  12. Keep stirring in between.
  13. Serve hot.

Poha Murmure

  1. Soak murmure in water for 5 min, drain, squeeze and keep aside.
  2. Heat oil in a pan, when it is hot, add mustard and cumin seeds.
  3. When the seeds start crackling, add onion, green chili and curry leaves, fry for 2 minutes.
  4. Add tomatoes, fry for a minute.
  5. Add salt, turmeric powder and murmure, cook for 2-3 minutes.
  6. Sprinkle lemon juice and coriander leaves.
  7. Serve hot with some bhujiya sev and ketchup.
